# Stone Creek Church — Brand Perspective
### A Guide for Drafting Social Media & Communications Content

---

## Who We Are

Stone Creek Church is a non-denominational church in Champaign-Urbana, IL, with a congregation of 2,500 people. We are a community of real people navigating real life — and we believe navigating life is easier when we follow our GPS: **gather, pray, serve.**

We exist to help people encounter a living God, grow in community, and overflow into the world around them. God's work in any one of us is never just for us — it's meant to overflow and impact others.

---

## Core Values

We consistently choose:

- **Simple** over Overdone
- **Generosity** over Obligation
- **Curiosity** over Certainty
- **Celebration** over Criticism
- **Innovation** over Cliché
- **Initiative** over Timidity
- **Invitation** over Exclusion

These values shape every word we write and every graphic we design. If a piece of content feels heavy, insider-coded, or performance-driven — it's off-brand.

---

## Brand Communication Standards

Our communication must be:

- **Culturally Relevant** — we speak the language of everyday life, not the language of a church bulletin
- **Tech Savvy** — we show up well across digital platforms and social channels
- **Everyday in language** — no seminary vocabulary, no Christianese, no clichés
- **Customized for its audience** — a Sunday invite post reads differently than a mid-week devotional

## Voice & Tone

### The North Star: Ricky's Voice

Our lead pastor's voice is the clearest model for how Stone Creek communicates. Content should feel like it came from a trusted friend — warm, honest, and grounded in Scripture — not from a press release or a religious institution.

**Core qualities:**

| Quality | What It Means in Practice |
|---|---|
| Warm without sentimentality | Encouraging, but not cheesy |
| Authority without ego | Confident, never condescending |
| Conviction without harshness | Clear truth, zero shame |
| Wisdom without complexity | Deep ideas in plain language |
| Humor without trying too hard | Disarming, not entertaining |

### Tone in Practice

**Conversational + Relatable**
Write like you're talking *with* someone, not *at* them.
> "Listen, I get it…" / "Can we be honest for a second?" / "Here's what I mean…"

**Pastoral + Encouraging**
Lift people up — even in correction.
> "God isn't trying to shame you. He's trying to set you free."

**Biblical + Clear**
Scripture is woven in naturally — never dropped in as a proof text.
> "Paul says it like this…" / "Jesus shows us in John 4…"

**Humorous + Human**
Quick, disarming, relatable. Never edgy or goofy.
> "If you're anything like me, by Tuesday you've already lost your Monday motivation."

## Cadence & Rhythm

Content should breathe. Short paragraphs. Spoken rhythm. Punchy emphasis.

**Ricky's structural pattern:**
1. Short, punchy sentences for clarity
2. Slightly longer lines for emotional build
3. A pause — let something land

**Rhetorical tools that work:**
- Repetition for emphasis
- Rhetorical questions for reflection
- Parallel structure to drive a point home

> *"We want God's promises, but we resist God's process.*
> *We want strength, but we avoid surrender.*
> *We want peace, but we won't put the phone down long enough to breathe."*

## Theological Posture

Our content consistently reflects these truths:

- **The Holy Spirit is transformative** — not flashy, but essential
- **God's presence is not optional** — it's the source of everything
- **Grace empowers change** — it's not a loophole
- **Spiritual disciplines matter** — prayer, Scripture, fasting, community
- **Healing happens together** — we don't do this alone
- **Everything flows back to Jesus** — He is the center

**We never use shame as a motivator.**
We always point toward freedom, identity, and formation.

### The Reframe
One of our most consistent moves: reframe struggle as invitation, not failure.

> "If you feel empty, that doesn't mean God left you. It means you're thirsty. And Jesus knows exactly what to do with thirsty people."

> "This is not God trying to take something from you — it's God making room."

> "Jesus meets you where you are, but He never leaves you there."

## Audience

We speak primarily to:

- 30- to 40-somethings
- Educated, suburban, midwestern
- People who may be de-churched or cautious about organized religion
- People who've been burned by "churchiness" — but are still spiritually open

**Because of this, we avoid:**
- Christian clichés and insider language
- Political commentary
- Fear-based messaging
- Over-the-top religious language

**Instead, we use:**
- Real life examples
- Practical next steps
- Emotional honesty
- Gentle humor
- Clear theology in everyday English

## Our GPS Framework

**Gather. Pray. Serve.** — this is how we describe spiritual life at Stone Creek.

- **Gather** — Community doesn't just happen. It's intentionally practiced. Neighborhood Gatherings are smaller communities across Champaign-Urbana where we don't just learn about faith, we live it.
- **Pray** — Prayer is not just something we do; it's an identity we embrace.
- **Serve** — God's work in us is always meant to overflow into others.

Content should consistently reflect these rhythms — not as programs to promote, but as a life to live together.

## Social Media Content Guardrails

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|---|---|
| Write like you're talking to one person | Write for a crowd |
| Use short, punchy sentences | Write paragraph walls with no breathing room |
| Lead with the human moment, then the truth | Lead with the Scripture reference |
| Celebrate what God is doing | Criticize cultural moments |
| Invite curiosity | Assume everyone's already in |
| Reference prayer, community, and the Spirit naturally | Make church sound like a program to attend |
| Use everyday language | Use Christianese or seminary vocabulary |
| Be encouraging in correction | Use shame as a motivator |
| Let Scripture land in context | Drop references in without connection |

---

## Signature Phrases to Reference

These moves appear in our voice consistently. Use them sparingly and with intention:

- **"Can we be honest for a minute?"** — sets up authenticity
- **"Here's what I want you to see…"** — redirects or clarifies Scripture
- **"Lean in for this — don't miss it."** — emphasizes a spiritual truth
- **"This is not God trying to take something from you…"** — reframes correction as love
- **"Jesus meets you where you are, but He never leaves you there."** — core theological theme
- **"It's 4:29."** — shorthand for Spirit-led speech (Ephesians 4:29)

## What Good SCC Content Feels Like

You feel safer after reading it.

It's the kind of content that makes someone think: *I didn't know I needed to hear that today.* It's honest about the hard stuff without being dramatic. It points toward Jesus without being preachy. It makes the person on the outside feel like there's room for them here.

**The goal is never to perform for people who are already in. The goal is to invite people who are still on their way.**
