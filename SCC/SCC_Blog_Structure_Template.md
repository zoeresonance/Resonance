# Stone Creek Church — Blog Structure Template

Use this document as the structural blueprint when drafting blogs for Stone Creek Church.
Voice and tone instructions are handled separately. This covers format, architecture, and flow only.

## Blog Architecture

Every SCC blog follows this general arc:

### 1. Hook / Opening Scene
- **Length:** 1–3 short paragraphs
- **Function:** Pull the reader in with a relatable human moment, a tension, a universal question, or a vivid scene
- **Pattern options:**
  - Open with a personal story or anecdote (e.g., the marathon at mile 18)
  - Open with a felt need or cultural observation (e.g., the world's desperate search for joy)
  - Open with a direct, simple declaration that creates tension
- **What to avoid:** Starting with Scripture, a theological statement, or a church announcement — earn the reader's attention first

---

### 2. The Big Idea / Central Question
- **Length:** 1–3 lines, often set apart visually (bold, line break, or standalone paragraph)
- **Function:** Name the core question or tension the post will answer
- **Examples from blogs:**
  - *"So what does the Bible actually mean when it talks about joy?"*
  - *"So if we're going to run free, we have to understand how sin actually works."*
  - *"You don't get to choose if you'll hit a wall in life or faith."*
- **Pattern:** Often phrased as a question or a reframing statement

---

### 3. Biblical / Theological Grounding
- **Length:** 2–5 short sections, each with a subheading or clear break
- **Function:** Unpack the spiritual truth using Scripture, stories, and clear explanation
- **Structure patterns:**
  - **Contrast structure** — Two opposing paths or stories (e.g., Lucifer vs. Jesus in Philippians 2)
  - **List structure** — Named characteristics or stages (e.g., how sin entangles: comfortable, constrictive, convenient, crafty)
  - **Narrative structure** — Walk through a Bible story beat by beat (e.g., Rahab, Jericho)
  - **Stage/journey structure** — Faith as a progression with a wall or turning point
- **Subheading style:** Short, punchy, declarative (e.g., "What Biblical Joy Really Is" / "What Sin Really Is" / "The Wall Isn't a Failure—It's a Stage")
- **Scripture handling:** Quote it briefly, then immediately unpack it in plain language — never drop it and move on

---

### 4. Practical Application
- **Length:** 1–3 short sections or a short list
- **Function:** Land the truth in real, everyday life — what does this actually look like?
- **Pattern:** Often starts with "What does this look like?" or a similar transition
- **Tone:** Concrete, honest, non-prescriptive — give examples without being preachy
- **Examples:**
  - Money, rights, privilege (from the Joy post)
  - What has gotten comfortable? What has gotten close? (from the Run Free post)
  - Prayer, fasting, consecration (from the Wall post)

---

### 5. Closing Invitation / Challenge
- **Length:** 3–8 short paragraphs or stacked short lines
- **Function:** Leave the reader with something to carry — a question, a declaration, a next step
- **Patterns:**
  - End with 2 reflection questions (e.g., "What did Jesus give up to serve you? / What might you need to give up to serve others?")
  - End with a direct challenge or call to action (e.g., "Hang your rope of hope.")
  - End with a restatement of the Big Idea, reframed with new depth
  - End with a memorable, quotable line (often the title restated or inverted)
- **What to avoid:** Summarizing everything you just said — the close should feel like an opening, not a wrap-up

---

## Formatting Rules

| Element | Guideline |
|---|---|
| Paragraph length | 1–4 sentences max. White space is structural. |
| Subheadings | Short, bold, sentence-case. Used every 150–300 words. |
| Pull-out lines | Key quotes or turning-point phrases set on their own line, sometimes italicized or bolded |
| Scripture | Always bolded when quoted directly. Kept brief. |
| Lists | Used sparingly — only when naming multiple parallel items (e.g., how sin works). Max 5 items. |
| Word count | 500–900 words for standard posts. Long-form up to ~1,100 words. |
| Images | One hero image per post (above the body copy). No inline images. |

## Recurring Structural Moves

These patterns appear consistently across SCC blogs and should be used intentionally:

- **The contrast pair** — Two stories, two paths, two characters set side by side to illuminate the truth
- **The definition reframe** — "Most people think X is about ___, but at its core it's ___."
- **The named list** — A short set of parallel characteristics (often 3–5), each with a one-line explanation
- **The rhetorical question close** — End sections or the whole post with a question that lands in the reader's chest
- **The title payoff** — The title's core phrase is restated or inverted in the closing line for resonance

---

## What Every SCC Blog Is NOT

- A sermon transcript or outline dump
- A listicle ("5 Ways to Have More Joy")
- A news article or announcement dressed up as a devotional
- A wall of text with no breathing room
- A guilt trip disguised as spiritual challenge
