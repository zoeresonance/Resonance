# Quote-Style Instagram Posts

A guide for making Stonecreek quote posts that feel cohesive without ever feeling repetitive.

**Format:** 1080 × 1350 (4:5 portrait — the IG feed default)
**Reference final:** `posts/pastor-ricky-pray-final.svg`
**HTML source:** `posts/pastor-ricky-pray.html`

---

## The recipe (what makes one of these "ours")

Every post in this style has six layers, in this stacking order:

1. **Solid color ground** — one of the brand greens or pebble white, never a photo, never a gradient as the primary fill.
2. **Two hollow rings** — thin stroke, anchored to opposite edges of the canvas. One large, one small.
3. **A big oversized open-quote glyph (`"`)** — Recoleta, in an accent color, sitting just above the quote. This is the visual hook.
4. **The quote itself** — Recoleta Regular, lowercase, set on multiple lines with deliberate line breaks.
5. **One emphasized word** — set in a different color (Sand, Tangerine, or a kids/youth accent) so the eye lands on the operative idea.
6. **Attribution** — Helvetica Neue, lowercase, prefixed with an em-dash. Sits in the bottom 15% of the canvas.

If a post has all six, it's on-system. If it's missing one, it'll feel like a different brand.

## What to vary (so they don't all look identical)

### 1. Background color — pick one per post
The post takes its mood from the ground.

| Color | Use it for |
|---|---|
| **Mossy Green** `#515F5D` | Default. Sermon quotes, weekly. Reverent + grounded. |
| **Moss Dark** `#3F4B49` | Heavier subjects — grief, lament, hard truths. |
| **Pebble White** `#F0EFE7` | Lighter takes, encouragement, simple statements. |
| **Desert Sand** `#B39986` | Warm, hospitality-flavored quotes. Use sparingly. |
| **Black** `#1B1A18` | YTH-flavored quotes only. Pair with Punch Yellow. |
| **Pebble White** + **Sunny Glow** rings | KIDS-flavored quotes. Pair with Tangerine emphasis. |

**Rotate through these.** Two Mossy Green posts in a row is fine; four in a row reads as a stuck template.

### 2. Ring placement — never the same twice in a series
The two rings always sit on opposite edges of the canvas, but where exactly they sit should change post to post.

Pick one large ring + one small ring, and place each in one of these positions:

- top-right, clipped (most common — see the final)
- top-left, clipped
- bottom-right, clipped
- bottom-left, clipped
- floating fully visible in a corner of negative space

Rules:
- **Never both on the same edge.** They balance the composition.
- **Never overlapping the type.** If a ring would touch a letter, move it or shrink it.
- **Stroke is always thin** — 3px on the 1080 canvas (~1.5px visual). Don't go thicker.

### 3. Ring colors — stay in the green family, with sand as alt
- Most often: **Moss Mid `#6C7E7C`** or **Sand `#B39986`** on a dark ground
- On Pebble grounds: **Moss Mid** or **Mossy Green**
- Mix it up — one ring in Sand, one in Moss Mid is great
- KIDS posts: rings in **Sunny Glow** or **Aqua Splash**
- YTH posts: rings in **Punch Yellow** `#E1FF83`

### 4. Which word gets the emphasis color
The emphasized word is what makes each post _hit_. Pick the operative word — usually a verb or the surprising noun.

- "the busier we are, the more we need to **pray**" → action
- "you're already **welcome** here" → the word that resolves the tension
- "patience allows you to celebrate small **victories**" → the unexpected noun

Don't emphasize the obvious word. Don't emphasize more than one word.

**Color of the emphasis:**
- On greens: **Sand** `#B39986` (warm against cool)
- On Pebble: **Mossy Green** `#515F5D` (depth) or **Tangerine** `#FE5000` (punch)
- On Black (YTH): **Punch Yellow** `#E1FF83`
- On Pebble (KIDS): **Tangerine Burst** `#FE5000`

### 5. Quote line breaks
**Always break the quote manually.** Don't let it auto-wrap. Set 3–5 lines, with each line completing a thought-fragment. Read it out loud — if a break makes you stumble, move it.

The emphasized word usually gets its own line, larger or alone, as the visual climax.

### 6. Top brand zone — vary which mark you use
Pick one per post:
- The **symbol mark** alone (`assets/symbol-pebble-white.svg`, recolor to suit)
- The **horizontal lockup** (smaller, top-left)
- Just `@stonecreek` as text (most minimal)
- For KIDS or YTH, use those sub-brand lockups instead

### 7. Footer line — optional, rotate
The bottom of the post can carry a small label. Rotate through:
- "sunday · stonecreek"
- "go together." (only when the quote is about community/movement — see voice guide)
- The sermon series name in lowercase
- The date
- Nothing — sometimes silence is correct

## Type sizing (don't go below these)

These are minimums for IG mobile readability. The 1080 canvas displays at ~400px wide on a phone.

| Element | Min size | What we used in the Ricky post |
|---|---|---|
| Quote (main lines) | 80px | 96px |
| Quote (emphasized word) | 96px | 140–160px (its own line, oversized) |
| Open-quote glyph | 180px | 220px |
| Attribution `— name` | 36px | 44px |
| @handle | 28px | 32px |
| Footer label | 26px | 30px |
| Symbol mark height | 48px | 56px |

**Below these = unreadable on a phone. Don't ship it.**

## The casing rule

Lowercase everything **except**:
- **God, Jesus, Lord, Holy Spirit, Father, Son, Christ** — always capitalized
- **Proper nouns** — names of people, books of the Bible, place names, ministry names (Pastor Ricky, Romans, Stonecreek KIDS)
- Nothing else — including the start of sentences, post titles, attributions, footers

Yes, this means "— pastor ricky" not "— Pastor Ricky." It's the system.

## Voice — what makes a quote postable

Pull from the brand voice guide (`SCC/Voice/stonecreek-brand-voice.md`). A good quote post:

- Lands in **under 14 words**, ideally under 10
- Uses **everyday language** — no jargon, no "fellowship," no "blessed and highly favored"
- **Asks a question or names a tension** rather than wrapping things up neatly
- Has **one operative word** that does the work — that's the word you emphasize
- **Sounds like something a thoughtful person would say to a friend**, not a sermon clip

If a quote needs setup to land, it's not ready for this format. Cut until the line stands on its own.

---

## Quick checklist before posting

- [ ] Background is one of the approved colors
- [ ] Two hollow rings, opposite edges, thin stroke (3px)
- [ ] No ring touches or covers any letter
- [ ] Quote is lowercase (except God / Jesus / proper nouns)
- [ ] One word is in an accent color
- [ ] Quote is under ~14 words and breaks cleanly
- [ ] All text is at or above the minimum sizes above
- [ ] Attribution starts with `— ` (em-dash + space)
- [ ] Symbol mark or wordmark is somewhere top
- [ ] When viewed at IG mobile size, every word is comfortably readable

## File starter

Duplicate `posts/pastor-ricky-pray.html` (or `.svg` for Illustrator). Change:
- The quote text + emphasized word
- The background color (CSS `--bg` on `.post`)
- Ring positions + colors
- The attribution
- The footer label

Save with a descriptive filename: `posts/pastor-ricky-{topic}.html` or `posts/{series}-{topic}.html`.
